Nexus Technologies is a San Francisco-based artificial intelligence company
founded in 2021 by Dr. Sarah Chen and Marcus Rivera.  The company specializes
in building enterprise knowledge graph platforms powered by large language models.
Dr. Chen serves as Chief Executive Officer while Rivera leads the engineering
division as Chief Technology Officer.  Their flagship product, NexusGraph, helps
Fortune 500 companies turn unstructured documents into queryable knowledge graphs.

The engineering team at Nexus Technologies relies heavily on Neo4j as their
primary graph database.  They use Python for their backend services and have
built a custom retrieval augmented generation pipeline called NexusRAG.  The
pipeline combines vector search with graph traversal to provide accurate answers
to complex enterprise queries.  NexusRAG uses Claude, Anthropic's large language
model, for both entity extraction and answer generation.

In March 2024 Nexus Technologies announced a strategic partnership with DataVault
Corporation, a cloud infrastructure provider headquartered in Seattle.  Under the
agreement DataVault provides dedicated GPU clusters for NexusRAG inference workloads
while Nexus Technologies integrates DataVault's storage APIs into the NexusGraph
platform.  The partnership was led by DataVault's VP of Partnerships, Kenji Tanaka,
and Nexus's Head of Business Development, Priya Sharma.

The company recently opened a research lab in London headed by Dr. James Okafor,
a former professor of computational linguistics at Imperial College London.
The London lab focuses on multilingual entity extraction and cross-lingual knowledge
graph alignment.  Dr. Okafor's team collaborates closely with the Natural Language
Processing group at Stanford University, where Dr. Chen completed her PhD in 2018.

Nexus Technologies has raised a total of 85 million dollars across three funding
rounds.  Their Series B was led by Horizon Ventures with participation from
TechFront Capital and Anthropic's corporate venture fund.  The company currently
employs 120 people across its San Francisco headquarters, Seattle satellite office,
and London research lab.
